A Word From IT

This document is provided to employees by the IT Department as an informational tool. This document includes the information that an employee needs to know about their computer system and the rights that they have on the company network. The information below is subject to change with notice after the fact and it is the responsibility of the employee to read and understand what is herein. For assistance accessing any of the services outlined below, please contact the helpdesk. 


 

Usernames & Passwords    

Your username is comprised of your first initial and your last name. If someone in the company already has those characters as their username, a number will be added to the end of yours to make it unique. Your username and password will be used to access all company resources and services. Your username will be provided by your manager upon employment. It will be in the format of your first initial followed by last name. For example, John Smith’s username would be “jsmith” (Without the quotes). For users with last names longer than 18 characters, the last name will be cut off after the 18th character. Users with duplicate usernames will have their username appended with a number, for example Jane Smith’s username would be “jsmith2” (Without the quotes). Your manager will provide you this information.

Your first login will be with a temporary password. At first login, you will be prompted to your password to one of your liking. This password needs to meet the password complexity requirements outlined below. It is against company policy to share your password with someone else.

Password Requirements:

  • The password contains characters from at least three of the following five categories:
    • English uppercase characters (A - Z)
    • English lowercase characters (a - z)
    • Base 10 digits (0 - 9)
    • Non-alphanumeric (For example: !, $, #, or %)
  • The password does not contain three or more contiguous characters from the user's account name.
  • Passwords must be at least 8 characters long
  • Passwords will expire every 90 days

·         The 3 previously used passwords cannot be reused

Home Folders

On Windows clients, your user home folders are available on your machine under My Computer (in Windows XP or below) or Computer (in Windows Vista and above) through the Z: drive.

On Unix-based systems, your home folder is automatically available as your main home directory. It can be accessed by navigating via the “cd” command with no arguments on the command line.

Directories and files in this folder belong to you and can be created, changed, and used like any other file and the folder in the system. The information in your home directory will automatically be transferred to the machine you are accessing after you log on.

Figure 2: User home folder available via the Z: drive (highlighted in blue)

 

Department Shares

Department shares are folders specific to each department. Access to these is limited by department. The department shares will be used for storing files that are cleared for viewing by anyone within your department. The department shares can also be used as a convenient means to transfer a single file to many members of your department. The department shares are NOT meant for storing personal files, and it would be best to use the chat client to transmit a single file to a single colleague.

The department shares will be mounted by default when you log into your system on both Windows and Linux machines. The department shares are stored on a centralized company server and shared out to every machine within the company. On Windows machines the department shares will be accessible under the My Computer (in Windows XP) or Computer (in Windows Vista and 7 icons. It is also available through the ‘Y:’ drive. On Unix-Based systems the department shares will be accessed by running “cd /ouShares”. This will change your directory to the /ouShares directory. You will only have access to the share for your department, and not any other department’s share. These files are automatically backed-up to protect employee data.

Internal FTP

While home directories and department folders and files are shared through the network, FTP will be used to directly transfer files to those inside the company.

Every system will come with the Filezilla FTP client program installed. When the program is fully initialized, you will see the "Quick connect" bar at the top of the program. Here, you will enter ftpinternal.eastco.sa3 as the host where the FTP server is, your username, and your password. An example of these fields can be seen below.

Figure 5: Connect to the FTP server through the Quickconnect bar

When you are successfully connected, the right explorer pane will bring you to the company-wide FTP folder. Here you can transfer files from your system into the folder to share with others in the company. Likewise, you can drag and drop files from the FTP folder into your local folder. When you transfer a file to your local system, you can manipulate it just like any other file/folder on the system.

Please note that ANY company employee can view these files stored on the FTP server.

VPN

All laptops that are distributed to employees will be preconfigured for the VPN. To create the VPN connection yourself, see the “Creating the Connection” section below.

Connecting on Windows 7

By the system clock in the bottom right corner of your screen, there should be an icon that either looks like a miniature white computer monitor or vertical signal bars. You may need to click on the upward-facing arrow to see it. Click on the icon and a list of connections will appear. Find the one labeled EastCo VPN and select it. The option to connect will appear; click it.

A window will open asking for your username, password and domain. Enter in your username and password and use the domain ad.eastco.sa3. Then click connect.

Chat

Spark should be installed on your computer by default. This program will allow you to chat with other employees. Open this program and by default a login screen will appear. The screen will have boxes for username, password, and server. It will ask you to save your password, and you will click on the checkbox to say yes. The username will be your username, usually first initial, last name, and the server will be chat.eastco.sa3.

Figure 15: Login with your username and password through the chat.eastco.sa3 server

Once this has been put in, the chat client will be properly functioning, and you can start chats with other people or add contacts using their username. Using the application is similar to other chat applications you should be familiar with.

Figure 16: The program has famliar chat functionality found in other chat applications

If you cannot authenticate, try the following:

  • Confirm network connectivity by going to the intranet site at http://intranet.eastco.sa3
  • Ensure you are typing your username and password correctly
  • The server name is correct.

If you are still unable to login, please contact the IT department.
